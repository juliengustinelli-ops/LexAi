The Problem

Your team is prospecting. They're just doing it blind.

Contact data is cheap. Context is what closes deals — and most teams never have enough of it.

Research takes hours per prospect

Finding a contact is 10 minutes. Understanding their org, budget cycle, tech stack, and buying timeline is 4 hours. Most reps skip it and go in with a generic pitch. Response rates reflect that.

What's inside a Full Intelligence Pack

The two top prospects get the full treatment. Here's exactly what that means.

01 — Org Chart + Decision Maker Map

Who actually buys — and who blocks

We map the full buying committee: Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Evaluator, and any known blockers. Each contact gets a personalization anchor and a recommended approach strategy.

02 — BANT + MEDDIC Scorecard

Do they have the budget, authority, and urgency?

We score Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline signals — and layer in MEDDIC: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Pain, and Champion. Every score is backed by a cited source.

03 — Live Buying Signal Analysis

Why this company, why now

Job postings, leadership changes, platform migrations, press releases, funding events, award announcements — we surface the signals that mean something is about to happen, and explain what they imply for your pitch.

04 — Competitive Positioning

What they use today and how to displace it

We identify their current vendors from job postings, LinkedIn, and tech signals — and give you the positioning angle that exploits gaps in their existing stack. No generic pitches.

05 — Ready-to-Send Outreach Copy

Copy. Paste. Send.

A personalized LinkedIn message and two email options — written for this specific prospect, referencing real signals, under 100 words each. Two subject line variants for A/B testing. Not a template with brackets.

06 — Cited Sources Throughout

Every fact is verifiable

Every claim — titles, signals, tech stack, org structure — is sourced inline and listed in a Sources table at the end. You know exactly where each piece of intelligence came from before you use it.
