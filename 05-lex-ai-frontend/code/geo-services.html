Sample Output

What findings look like — real example

Actual findings from a GEO audit we ran on a B2B SaaS site in March 2026.

● Critical

GPTBot and ClaudeBot are blocked in robots.txt

The site's robots.txt file contains a Disallow: / directive for GPTBot and ClaudeBot — the crawlers that feed ChatGPT and Claude. This means the site is completely invisible to two of the three largest AI search platforms. Fix: Remove the block and add an explicit llms.txt file to guide AI crawlers to priority content.

● High Priority

No FAQ or HowTo schema on any page — missing AI answer box eligibility

The site has 14 pages with question-based content but none have FAQ or HowTo schema markup. Google's AI Overviews and Bing Copilot heavily favor structured content for answer boxes. Adding schema to these 14 pages is the highest-leverage quick win available — estimated 2–3 day implementation.

● High Priority

Homepage content scores 22/100 for AI citability — no extractable answer blocks

The homepage copy is written in marketing language rather than answer-format language. AI models look for self-contained passages that directly answer a question. The homepage has zero passages that meet this threshold. Rewriting 3–4 key sections in answer format would raise citability to ~65/100.

● Medium Priority

Brand mentioned on LinkedIn but absent from Reddit, G2, and industry forums

AI models weight brand mentions across multiple platforms when deciding whether to cite or recommend a brand. The site has good LinkedIn activity but no presence on Reddit, G2, or industry-specific forums — all of which are heavily indexed by AI training datasets.
