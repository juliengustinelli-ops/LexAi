## Post Structure (Template)

Every post follows this structure:

1. **Headline** — keyword-first, specific, outcome-driven
2. **Intro** — hook in 2-3 sentences, state the problem
3. **The Problem** — why this matters, who it affects
4. **The Solution / How-To** — meat of the post, specific and actionable
5. **Real Example** — tie back to a LexAi case study where relevant
6. **Key Takeaways** — 3-5 bullet summary (featured snippet bait)
7. **CTA** — "Want this built for your business? Book a free call."

---

## Guidelines (from Julien)

- **Tone:** Conversational peer-to-peer — write like explaining something useful to a colleague, not pitching a product. No buzzwords, no fluff.
- **Length:** 1,000–1,200 words for how-to and versus posts. Up to 1,500 for case study posts.
- **Voice:** First person "we" — "We built a lead engine that..." feels more credible and human than third person.
- **Avoid:**
  - Overpromising ("AI will transform your business forever")
  - Generic AI hype language ("leverage cutting-edge AI to unlock synergies")
  - Naming client revenue or deal sizes — stick to operational metrics (hours saved, lead quality %, users)
  - ChatGPT-sounding writing — no em-dash overuse, no "in today's fast-paced world"
- **Primary CTA:** Book a Free Call — end every post with: *"If you want this built for your business, book a free 30-minute call — no pitch, just a conversation about what's possible."*
- **Exception:** GEO audit posts use the GEO audit CTA instead of book a call.
