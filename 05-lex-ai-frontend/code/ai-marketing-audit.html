Sample Output

What the findings look like

Every finding is severity-rated and includes the specific fix — not just the problem. Here's a sample from a real B2B SaaS marketing audit.

● Critical

7 of 12 blog posts are targeting informational keywords with a sales page structure

These posts rank for "how to" queries (informational intent) but are structured as product pages with CTAs above the fold and minimal educational content. Google's ranking systems classify these as intent mismatches — they suppress rankings and inflate bounce rate. Fix: restructure the top 3 posts as genuine how-to guides with the CTA at the end. Estimated effort: 4–6 hours per post.

● High Priority

Homepage title tag is 78 characters and missing the primary keyword in the first 50

The current title tag is truncated in search results and leads with the brand name rather than the primary keyword. For a site without strong brand recognition, this reduces click-through rate significantly on competitive queries. Fix: rewrite to lead with the primary keyword, keep under 60 characters. This is a 5-minute change with measurable CTR impact within 2–4 weeks.

● Medium Priority

No internal links from high-traffic blog posts to service pages

Three blog posts averaging 400+ monthly visitors have zero internal links to the site's core service pages. These posts are building topical authority but not passing it to the pages that convert. Adding 2–3 contextual internal links per post would improve both rankings for the service pages and conversion path clarity for visitors who arrive through search.
