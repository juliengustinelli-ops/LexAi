# Are You Treating Writing Like an Afterthought in Your Marketing?

**A book review of "Everybody Writes" by Ann Handley**

Blog posts that say nothing. Emails that feel robotic. Landing pages that sound like they were written by committee.

If your marketing content isn't converting, the problem might not be your strategy—it might be your writing.

## About the Author

Ann Handley is a digital marketing pioneer and Chief Content Officer at MarketingProfs. She's the author of multiple bestsellers and is widely recognized as one of the most influential voices in content marketing.

## What the Book Covers

"Everybody Writes" breaks down writing into a learnable, repeatable skill. Here are the core principles:

1. **Writing with purpose and clear intent** — Every piece of content should have a specific goal
2. **Prioritizing clarity over cleverness** — Simple language beats fancy words
3. **Developing consistent voice and tone** — Your brand should sound like one person
4. **Understanding editing as the real writing work** — First drafts are just the beginning

## Three Big Lessons

✅ **Writing is a business skill, not just a creative one.** Whether you're a marketer, founder, or freelancer, your ability to communicate clearly affects your bottom line.

✅ **Small improvements compound.** You don't need to become a literary genius. Getting 10% better at headlines, emails, and calls-to-action adds up fast.

✅ **Empathy drives effective messaging.** The best writing isn't about you—it's about understanding what your reader needs to hear.

## Why Read It?

If you write anything for your business—emails, social posts, landing pages, proposals—this book gives you a framework to do it better. It's practical, actionable, and refreshingly free of fluff.

Stop treating writing like an afterthought. It might be the highest-leverage skill you can develop.
